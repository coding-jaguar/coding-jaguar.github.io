Analog Circuits

Course Overview

Before the three decades, design based on digital circuits are widely used because of more flexibility and reliability. There are no doughs that any analog functions are implemented in the digital domain, for example, digital radio. However, the physical world is inherently analog. indicating that there will always need for analog circuits to condition the physical signals such as associated with transducers and data conversion. Also, new applications like wireless communication continue to emerge where speed and power make more used in the analog front end design. Many applications today mixed mode ICs where analog circuit interface with the physical world and digital circuit for processing and control. However, the analog circuit contains a small area on chip (about 20%), it is a more challenging part of the design, affect the overall performance of the system. This course introduces design and analysis of analog Circuits using an operational amplifier. The different challenges and issues are identified in designing of analog circuit as compared with digital design. The course includes basic op-amp configurations, linear and non-linear applications of the op-amp and active filter synthesis, including timer ICs, Phase Locked Loop ICs, Voltage regulators and power amplifiers.

Course Outcomes

  1. Comprehend the fundamentals of the op-amp and its basic amplifier configurations
  2. Analyze the linear and non-linear applications of the operational amplifier
  3. Design and Construct various circuits using operational amplifier based ICs.
